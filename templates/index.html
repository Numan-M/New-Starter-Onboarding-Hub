{% if current_item == 'Welcome' %}

This site contains a checklist of tasks for new starters. Use the menu on the left to navigate through the onboarding steps. Click "Mark Complete" on each page once you've completed the step, and feel free to revisit any section at any time.

If you have any questions, don't hesitate to reach out to your manager.

{% elif current_item == 'Training' %}

You will need to complete training modules assigned to you through MyLearningHub and additional classroom training sessions which you will be notified about.

Training will include health and safety, compliance, role-specific training, and more. Please check MyLearningHub regularly to stay on top of your assigned courses and deadlines.

You also have access to a range of resources to support your learning and development, these resources include:

  • LinkedIn Learning: Access a vast library of online courses and tutorials to enhance your skills and knowledge.
  • QA: Access courses and training materials to enhance your tech stack.
  • Internal documentation: Explore our internal knowledge base for guides, best practices, and company-specific information.

Once you've completed your training, you can mark this step as complete. If you have any questions about your training requirements, please contact your manager or HR representative.

{% elif current_item == 'Authentication' %}

Setting up authentication correctly ensures you can securely access all company systems from day one.

You will need to complete the following:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enrol your device with the company's MFA system. You will receive an email with setup instructions. MFA is required to access most internal systems.
  • Single Sign-On (SSO): Once MFA is set up, most tools and applications will be accessible via SSO using your company credentials.
  • Password policy: Ensure your password meets the company's requirements — minimum 12 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Passwords must be changed every 90 days.
  • VPN: Install and configure the company VPN to access internal systems when working remotely. Setup instructions will be provided by IT.

If you encounter any issues during setup, raise a ticket with the IT helpdesk or contact your manager.

{% elif current_item == 'Equipment' %}

Your equipment should have been arranged prior to your start date. Please check that you have received everything you need to work effectively.

  • Laptop: Your laptop should be pre-configured with the necessary software. If anything is missing or not working, contact IT.
  • Building access: Your access card or credentials for building entry should be ready on your first day. Contact facilities if you have any issues.

If you require any additional equipment, you can place a request through MyIT.

If any equipment is missing, damaged, or not functioning correctly, raise a ticket with the IT helpdesk as soon as possible.

{% elif current_item == 'Information sources' %}

There are several key platforms and resources you should familiarise yourself with to stay informed and find the information you need.

  • Intranet The company intranet is your central hub for news, policies, announcements, and internal resources. Bookmark it and check it regularly.
  • Confluence Our internal knowledge base where teams document processes, technical guides, and project information. You will be granted access as part of the Access checklist.
  • SharePoint Used for storing and sharing documents across teams. Your manager will point you to the relevant team sites.
  • Company newsletter: A regular update covering company news, initiatives, and events. You will be automatically subscribed using your company email.
  • Team meetings: Regular team stand-ups and all-hands meetings are a key source of up-to-date information. Your manager will add you to the relevant calendar invites.

If you are unsure where to find something, your manager or a colleague will be able to point you in the right direction.

{% elif current_item == 'Organisation structure' %}

Understanding how the organisation is structured will help you navigate teams, escalate issues, and collaborate effectively.

  • Your team: Your direct team, reporting line, and key contacts will be shared with you by your manager during your first week.
  • Departments: The company is organised into several departments including Technology, Operations, Finance, HR, and more. Each department has its own leadership team.
  • Leadership: An overview of the senior leadership team is available on the intranet. Familiarise yourself with key stakeholders relevant to your role.
  • Org chart: A full organisational chart is available on the intranet and is updated regularly. Your manager can walk you through the structure most relevant to your team.

Don't hesitate to ask your manager or colleagues if you are unsure about who to contact for a particular matter.

{% elif current_item == 'myHR' %}

myHR is the self-service HR platform where you can manage your personal information, payroll, and leave.

You should log in and complete the following as soon as possible:

  • Personal details: Verify that your name, address, and contact information are correct.
  • Bank details: Enter your bank account details to ensure your salary is paid correctly.
  • Tax information: Review and confirm your tax code. If anything looks incorrect, contact payroll.
  • Emergency contact: Add at least one emergency contact.
  • Leave: Familiarise yourself with how to request annual leave, view your entitlement, and check your remaining balance.

Access myHR using your company SSO credentials. If you have any issues logging in or completing your details, contact HR directly.

{% elif current_item == 'Employee benefits' %}

The company offers a range of benefits available to all employees. Take time to review what is available and enrol where required.

  • Pension: You will be automatically enrolled into the company pension scheme. Details of contribution rates and how to manage your pension are available on the intranet.
  • Private healthcare: Eligible employees can enrol in the company's private healthcare scheme. Check the intranet or contact HR for eligibility and enrolment details.
  • Annual leave: Your leave entitlement is outlined in your contract. Leave is managed through myHR.
  • Cycle to work scheme: Save on the cost of a bike and accessories through the government-backed cycle to work scheme. Details are available on the intranet.
  • Employee assistance programme (EAP): Confidential support for personal and professional challenges, available 24/7. Contact details are on the intranet.
  • Learning & development budget: Depending on your role, you may have access to a personal L&D budget. Speak to your manager for details.

For full details on all benefits and how to enrol, visit the benefits section of the intranet or contact HR.

{% elif current_item == 'Jira' %}

Jira is our project and issue tracking tool, used across teams to manage work, track bugs, and plan sprints.

To request access:

  • Raise a request through the IT helpdesk via ServiceNow, selecting Jira from the application access list.
  • Your manager will need to approve the request and confirm which projects you require access to.
  • Once approved, you will receive a confirmation email and can log in via SSO.

Once you have access, your manager or team lead will walk you through the boards and workflows relevant to your role.

{% elif current_item == 'Confluence' %}

Confluence is our internal knowledge base and documentation platform. Teams use it to document processes, technical guides, decisions, and project information.

To request access:

  • Raise a request through ServiceNow, selecting Confluence from the application access list.
  • Your manager will confirm which spaces you need access to.
  • Once approved, log in using your SSO credentials.

Start by exploring your team's space and any spaces your manager recommends. Confluence is a great first port of call when looking for how something works or has been documented previously.

{% elif current_item == 'Figma' %}

Figma is our design and prototyping tool, used by design and product teams to create and collaborate on UI designs.

To request access:

  • Raise a request through ServiceNow, selecting Figma from the application access list.
  • Access is typically granted at viewer level by default. If you require editor access, your manager will need to confirm this in the request.
  • Once approved, you will receive an invitation to join the company Figma organisation via your company email.

If your role does not require Figma access, you can skip this step.

{% elif current_item == 'ServiceNow' %}

ServiceNow is our IT service management platform. It is used to raise and track IT support tickets, request software access, and report incidents.

To request access:

  • ServiceNow access is provisioned automatically for all new starters. You should be able to log in using your SSO credentials from your first day.
  • If you cannot access ServiceNow, contact the IT helpdesk directly by phone or email — details are on the intranet.

You will use ServiceNow to request access to most of the other tools in this checklist, so getting this set up first is important.

{% elif current_item == 'Service accounts' %}

Depending on your role, you may require one or more service accounts to access shared systems, run automated processes, or integrate with external services.

  • What is a service account: A service account is a non-personal account used by applications or automated processes rather than individual users.
  • Requesting a service account: Raise a request through ServiceNow with details of the system and purpose. Your manager will need to approve the request.
  • Credentials management: Service account credentials must be stored securely using the approved secrets management tool. Do not store credentials in code or plain text files.

If you are unsure whether your role requires a service account, speak to your manager or team lead.

{% elif current_item == 'Developer folders' %}

As a developer, you will need access to the relevant shared folders and repositories for your team.

  • SharePoint / network drives: Request access to your team's shared folders through ServiceNow. Your manager will confirm which folders are relevant to your role.
  • Code repositories: Access to the company's source control system (e.g. GitHub, Azure DevOps) will be arranged by your team lead. You will receive an invitation via your company email.
  • Naming conventions: Familiarise yourself with the team's folder structure and naming conventions — these are documented in Confluence.

Once access has been granted, your manager or a senior team member will walk you through the folder structure and where to find key resources.

{% elif current_item == 'Developer tools' %}

The following tools are commonly used by the development team. Ensure you have access to everything relevant to your role.

  • IDE: The team primarily uses Visual Studio Code. Download it from the official site if it is not already installed on your machine.
  • Azure DevOps / GitHub: Used for source control, CI/CD pipelines, and project tracking. Request access via ServiceNow.
  • Docker: Used for containerisation. Installation instructions and internal usage guidelines are available in Confluence.
  • Postman: Used for API testing. Available to download from the official site.
  • Azure Portal: Access to the Azure Portal is role-dependent. Request access via ServiceNow if required.

Your team lead will advise on any additional tools specific to your project or tech stack. Internal setup guides for most tools are available in Confluence.
